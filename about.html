About

The Signal Archive is the first public project of Negentropy Lab. It records the output of a football betting prediction model over a defined operational period beginning April 2026.

The archive exists to establish a permanent, time-sealed record of a finite research project. Each signal is committed to the repository before the corresponding match begins; each settlement is recorded shortly afterward, alongside the decryption key that renders the original prediction auditable. Nothing in the archive can be altered after the fact without leaving a public trace.

The scope is fixed in advance. A predetermined set of evaluation windows will be observed; when the final window closes, the archive will be sealed and preserved in its terminal state. No further signals will be added after that point, and the site you are reading will continue to exist as a static record of what the model did and did not do.

The archive is designed for observation, not interaction. There is no service offering, no subscription, no membership, no consulting engagement. Those interested in the protocol should consult the Protocol page. Those interested in the data should consult the repository directly.
